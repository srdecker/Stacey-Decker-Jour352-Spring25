Women and Black Americans More Likely to be Diagnosed with Food Allergies

Although food allergies are relatively uncommon, there are disparities among disagnosed groups.

By Haley Parsley

CDC data from 2021 shows that only 6% of Americans are diagnosed with food allergies. This is a relatively small amount: comparatively, 26% of Americans are diagnosed with seasonal allergies.

The CDC's data about who experiences food allergies reveals gender and racial disparities in diagnosis rates. Notably, women are diagnosed with food allergies at twice the rate of men.
